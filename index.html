Know the Game

The whole sport, in five plain-English ideas.

  1. Three outs end a half-inning. Nine innings make a game — unless it's tied, then you play on.
  2. Four balls is a walk; three strikes is an out. The count rules every at-bat.
  3. Cross all four bases in order and you score a run. Touch them out of order and you don't.
  4. A fielder's choice, a sacrifice, a stolen base — small decisions decide close games.
  5. 162 games in a regular season. Baseball rewards the patient over the streaky.
